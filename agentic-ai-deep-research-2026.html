Agentic AI — The State of the Market, Mid-2026

Funding, company values, revenues and open-area analysis across the full industry · prepared 23 June 2026 · all figures gathered via live web search

This is a full sweep of the "agentic AI" opportunity space, taken purely as a theme. Every funding and valuation figure below was pulled from live web search and is referenced. The single big message: money is concentrating into a few giant companies, raw AI "intelligence" is becoming a cheap commodity, and the lasting value is shifting to the layers that make agents safe enough to actually use in production — trust, identity, memory, governance, testing — and to deep industry-specific products.

The main funding pattern: fewer but bigger bets. The number of deals has fallen while the average cheque size has nearly doubled — investors are concentrating their money into the established leaders. For a new entrant, this means the headline categories (coding, support, legal, medical notes) are practically closed unless you have a war-chest — while genuinely new and urgent categories can still attract first-time investment.

A word of caution: when Stripe, Google, Coinbase and Amazon are setting the standard, the basic "rail" itself is not a startup opportunity. The openings are around it — verification ("check first, pay later"), spending control, reconciliation, fraud detection, and buying-tools for merchants.

9Final takeaways

  1. Money is concentrating. Fewer but bigger deals (avg $155M) into the leaders. The headline categories (coding, support, voice, legal, medical notes) are closed to small teams.
  2. The "trust gap" is the richest infrastructure opening. 86% trial failure, 60% governance gap, only 7% confident in their agent controls → identity, governance, testing and security are funded and still have open niches (especially governance & agent-identity).
  3. Industry depth beats industry breadth. 54.6% of money is industry-specific, but each big industry already has two leaders. The room is in unglamorous, scattered, or old-system-bound industries (manufacturing, construction, procurement, retail operations, companies stuck on legacy systems).
  4. Do not build the "rail" — build beside it. The payment standards and the buying side are owned by Stripe/Google/Coinbase/Amazon. Verification, governance and merchant tools around them are open.
  5. The single best risk-adjusted shape for a lean team: a deep agent in a boring/old-system/regulated industry (a strong moat behind friction) — or a trust-gap supporting tool that the big players above will want to buy.
