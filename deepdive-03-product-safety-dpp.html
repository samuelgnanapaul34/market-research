01The problem & the wedge

Toys and children's products are among the most heavily regulated consumer categories — EN71 (EU), ASTM F963 + CPSIA (US), UKCA (UK). An importer/retailer must hold a valid, current safety document for every SKU and supplier, and prove it on demand for 10 years. The work is manual, document-heavy, and error-prone:

  • ~45% of factory-provided test reports are expired or issued for a different material — so the file is silently non-compliant.
  • Toys are ~21% of 2026 recalls (2nd-highest category) — the cost of getting it wrong is severe.
  • Three hard 2026 deadlines create urgency + budget: US CPSC certificate e-filing becomes mandatory 8 July 2026 (CBP); EU Toy Safety Reg 2025/2509 adds chemical rules + a Digital Product Passport for every toy model; GPSR puts liability + traceability on the importer/distributor.
The wedge: an agent that owns the supplier-document lifecycle — collect, validate (current? right material? right standard?), flag gaps, build the technical file, generate the CPC, e-file to CBP, and assemble the EU technical file + DPP. The compliance incumbents serve manufacturers at BOM level; nobody owns finished-goods document compliance for a multi-supplier retailer/importer.

02ICP & buyer

Who buys

  • Champion: Head of Product Safety / Technical / QA. Economic buyer: General Counsel / Head of Compliance or Buying Director.
  • Firmographics: importers & retailers of toys, juvenile products, and other regulated consumer goods selling into US/EU/UK; multi-supplier, Asia-sourced.
  • Trigger: the July-2026 e-filing mandate; an EU 2025/2509 readiness scramble; a recall or border hold.

Where they congregate (reachable)

  • Importer / product-compliance trade media; toy-industry associations.
  • Strong, dated search intent: "CPSC eFiling", "EN71 compliance", "Children's Product Certificate", "toy DPP".
  • Testing labs (UL, SGS, Eurofins, Bureau Veritas) & customs brokers = referral partners.

05Product & execution strategy

v1 — ride the deadline

  • Lead with the US CPSC e-filing wedge (mandatory 8 July 2026): ingest CPCs/test reports → validate → e-file to CBP. Sharp, dated, urgent.
  • Then the document-validation engine: flag expired / wrong-material / wrong-standard reports (the 45% problem).
  • Then EU technical file + DPP generation for 2025/2509.

Architecture & build

  • Supplier-doc ingestion (PDF/portal) → extraction + validation against standard & material → technical-file assembly → CPC/DoC generation → CBP e-file + DPP registration.
  • Build difficulty: moderate; the rules/standards engine is the moat.
  • Domain: codifiable via one toy-safety/compliance advisor + design-partner importers.

06Roadmap & timeline

0–3 mo · Deadline wedge. CPSC e-filing + CPC generation for US importers; doc-validation engine (expiry/material/standard); 3 design-partner importers. Time it to the July-2026 mandate.
3–6 mo · EU + DPP. EN71/UKCA technical file + Digital Product Passport generation for 2025/2509; supplier-portal for document collection; first paying importers.
6–12 mo · Scale. 15–30 importers; recall-readiness archive; integrations with testing-lab report feeds + customs brokers.
12–24 mo · Expand. Adjacent regimes (restricted substances, EPR) + broaden categories beyond toys (juvenile, electricals); 70–120 importers → £3–5M ARR.

09Pilot / 2-week bake-off

  • Setup: one importer, a subset of SKUs/suppliers, their current safety-document folder.
  • Run: agent validates each document (expiry/material/standard), flags gaps, drafts the CPCs + a CBP e-file package.
  • Success metric: % of documents correctly classified + non-compliant docs surfaced that the team missed; a ready-to-file CPC set.
  • Green-light: it finds real gaps + produces filings the compliance lead trusts.
