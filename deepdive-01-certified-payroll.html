Certified-Payroll Agent

An agent that generates, validates and files weekly certified-payroll reports (WH-347 + state equivalents) and enforces Davis-Bacon / prevailing-wage compliance — so government contractors never trigger withheld payment or debarment.

01The problem & the wedge

Any contractor on federally-funded (Davis-Bacon, contracts >$2,000) or state-funded ("little Davis-Bacon", ~26 states) construction must pay covered workers weekly and file a certified-payroll report every week — federal form WH-347 plus state-specific formats — proving correct prevailing wages and fringe benefits for every worker classification.

  • It's mandatory and unforgiving. A wrong or late filing means withheld payment, back-wage liability, and — repeat — debarment from public work. Existential, not annoying.
  • It's weekly and manual. Payroll clerks re-key hours into WH-347, compute fringe credits and apprenticeship ratios by hand, and reconcile to dozens of wage determinations.
  • Incumbents are legacy. LCPtracker, Points North, eBacon and friends are clunky desktop-era tools; users complain of manual entry and poor UX.
The wedge: a mandatory weekly filing + severe penalties + legacy non-AI incumbents + a tailwind (infrastructure spending expanded prevailing-wage work). The agent ingests the payroll register, computes fringe, fills the correct form per jurisdiction, validates against the wage determination, and files — turning a half-day weekly grind into minutes.

02ICP & buyer

Who buys

  • Champion + economic buyer often the same: the payroll / compliance manager (or owner at smaller firms) at a contractor doing public-works jobs.
  • Firmographics: construction / specialty-trade contractors, ~$2M–$250M revenue, 1–10 active prevailing-wage projects, US (federal + DBRA states).
  • Trigger: winning a public-works bid; an audit; a missed/penalised filing.

Where they congregate (reachable)

  • AGC & ABC chapters; SAM.gov / bid boards.
  • Clear search intent: "certified payroll software", "WH-347", "Davis-Bacon compliance".
  • Construction-payroll/ERP ecosystems (Foundation, Sage 300 CRE, Vista, ADP).
  • GovCon & construction trade media.

05Product & execution strategy

v1 — narrowest wedge

  • Federal WH-347 only, one payroll source (ADP / QuickBooks / Foundation export), top-5 prevailing-wage states.
  • Drop-in: upload payroll register → validated WH-347 + fringe statement in minutes.
  • Human-review mode first (draft for sign-off) to clear the trust bar before autonomous filing.

Architecture & build

  • Ingest payroll → map classifications to wage determinations → compute fringe credits → fill form templates → validate → file to agency portals (incl. LCPtracker/state e-CPR).
  • Build difficulty: moderate. The hard part is the rules engine (the moat), not the plumbing.
  • Domain knowledge: codifiable via one prevailing-wage compliance advisor + 3 design-partner contractors.

06Roadmap & timeline

0–3 mo · Wedge. Federal WH-347 generator + one payroll integration; rules engine for fringe; 3 design-partner contractors; free "WH-347 / compliance checker" as inbound magnet.
3–6 mo · Compliance brain. Top-5 state forms (CA DIR e-CPR, NY, IL, WA, NJ); apprenticeship-ratio + fringe validation; agency e-filing automation. First 10 paying contractors.
6–12 mo · Scale. 20–40 paying contractors; more state forms; audit-ready archive + violation flagging; integrations with construction ERPs.
12–24 mo · Expand. Adjacent compliance (SCA service-contract certified payroll, EEO/apprenticeship reporting, prevailing-wage for IRA clean-energy credits); 150+ contractors → £1M+ ARR run-rate.

09Pilot / 2-week bake-off

  • Setup: one contractor, one active Davis-Bacon project, last 4 weeks of payroll registers.
  • Run: agent produces the 4 weekly WH-347s + fringe statements; contractor's clerk reviews.
  • Success metric: first compliant WH-347 in <1 day (vs ~half-day manual/week), ≥95% field accuracy, zero rejected filings.
  • Green-light: clerk would trust it to draft every week → convert to paid.
