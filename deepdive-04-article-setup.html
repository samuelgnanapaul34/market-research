01The problem & the wedge

When a retailer ranges new products, someone has to create each SKU in the ERP — pulling attributes, barcodes, dimensions, classifications, safety data and pricing out of supplier line-sheets, PDFs and spreadsheets and re-keying them by hand. For a category with heavy seasonal newness (toys, fashion, general merchandise) that's thousands of new SKUs per season.

  • ~15–30 minutes per SKU, error-prone, across many supplier formats.
  • It's the #1 grind for assistant buyers — and a wrong or late setup means a missed on-shelf date.
  • Root cause: data lives in supplier emails/spreadsheets + the ERP with no single source of truth (duplicates, missing fields, stale records).
The wedge: an agent that reads any supplier line-sheet, extracts + enriches + classifies, and writes a clean article master into the ERP for one-click buyer approval. It's an authoring task on the system of record — high-volume, codifiable, and painful.

05Product & execution strategy

v1 — narrowest wedge

  • One category + one ERP: supplier line-sheet → drafted, enriched article master → buyer approves.
  • Win the "any supplier format → clean ERP record" reliability that generic PIM struggles with.

Architecture & build

  • Line-sheet ingestion (PDF/Excel) → extraction + attribute mapping + classification + enrichment → ERP article-master write (approval queue).
  • Build difficulty: moderate; the long tail of supplier formats + ERP write logic is the work.
  • Domain: article-master + merchandising data — codifiable with design partners.

06Roadmap & timeline

0–3 mo · Wedge. One ERP + one category; line-sheet → drafted article master; 3 design partners; measure minutes-saved + field-accuracy.
3–6 mo · Reliability. Broaden supplier formats; enrichment + classification quality; approval workflow; first paying retailers.
6–12 mo · Scale. Second ERP connector; more categories; QA/dedup; 15–25 retailers.
12–24 mo · Suite. Combine with PO-exception + onboarding on the same buyer → buyer-suite; 80–100 retailers → £4–5M ARR.

07GTM / distribution

Channels

  • ERP/PIM app marketplaces; retail-ops trade media.
  • Wedge-led inbound: a free "line-sheet → ERP article" demo on the buyer's own sample.
  • Outbound to high-newness retailers on a known ERP.

Motion & the GTM hire

  • Land via a time-saved proof on real supplier sheets; expand into the buyer-suite.
  • Domain GTM hire with a retail merchandising-ops network.
  • Differentiate explicitly vs PIM ("writes your ERP, not another silo") and vs Joule ("finished agent, no build project").

09Pilot / 2-week bake-off

  • Setup: one supplier's line-sheet(s) + the retailer's ERP article-master template.
  • Run: agent drafts enriched article records for buyer review.
  • Success metric: ≥70% of fields correct on first pass + ≥50% time saved per SKU vs manual.
  • Green-light: buyers would approve agent drafts rather than key from scratch.
