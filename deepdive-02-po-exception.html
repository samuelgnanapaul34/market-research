Retailer-Side PO-Exception Agent

An agent that watches a retailer's inbound purchase orders to suppliers, detects exceptions (short-ships, price/qty mismatches, late ASNs), and autonomously chases suppliers to resolve them — stopping chargebacks, AR leakage and avoidable stockouts.

01The problem & the wedge

A retailer raises thousands of POs to suppliers. A large share come back wrong — short-shipped, mis-priced, wrong quantities, late or missing ASNs, unconfirmed. Today the retailer's own ERP reports these passively; it doesn't chase the supplier. So buyers/supply-chain admins do it by hand over email and phone.

  • It's a daily grind: a 100-line PO can take 60–90 min to reconcile manually; exceptions pile up across hundreds of suppliers.
  • It costs real money: unresolved exceptions become chargebacks, accounts-receivable leakage, and on-shelf stockouts.
  • The incumbent is "a report": the ERP shows the discrepancy but does nothing about it; enterprise SCM suites that could are priced for tier-1 only.
The wedge: an agent that sits on the PO → ASN → goods-receipt flow, detects exceptions, and acts — drafting and sending the supplier chase, tracking the resolution, and reconciling. The supplier side of this market is crowded; the retailer side has no funded pure-play.

02ICP & buyer

Who buys

  • Champion: supply-chain / merchandising-ops manager. Economic buyer: Head of Supply Chain or Buying Director.
  • Firmographics: multi-supplier retailers & wholesale distributors, ~$50M–$5B revenue, on a real ERP (SAP/Oracle/Dynamics), PO-heavy, below tier-1 (RELEX/Blue Yonder priced them out).
  • Trigger: chargeback pain, AR write-offs, stockouts, a lean ops team drowning in exceptions.

Where they congregate

  • Retail / supply-chain trade media & events.
  • ERP app marketplaces (the system of record = the integration moat).
  • Supply-chain communities; search intent around chargebacks / OTIF / exception management.

05Product & execution strategy

v1 — the diagnostic wedge

  • Lead with a read-only "Exception-Loss Audit" on a top-supplier subset — produces the £ figure the buyer can't compute (overpaid invoices + avoidable stockouts + unclaimed penalties).
  • Zero agency = easy first yes; then convert to the agent that recovers it.

Architecture & build

  • Read-only feed: PO / ASN (856) / goods-receipt / supplier email → detect → draft supplier chase → track → reconcile (write-back later).
  • Build difficulty: moderate; ERP integration is the friction (and the moat).
  • Domain: retail-supplier ops — codifiable with design partners.
The real risk is the sale, not the tech. Many mid-market retailers can't quantify their own exception losses (one study: only 3 of 11 could). So the audit is the sales instrument — sell the diagnosis first.

06Roadmap & timeline

0–3 mo · Diagnostic. Read-only Exception-Loss Audit on one ERP; 2-week pilot spec; 3 design partners; measure touchless-resolution rate.
3–6 mo · The agent. Autonomous supplier chasing + resolution tracking; one ERP integration deep; first paying retailers. Green-light pilots at ≥40–50% touchless.
6–12 mo · Scale & moat. Second ERP connector; resolution-trace graph compounding; write-back/reconciliation; 15–30 retailers.
12–24 mo · Suite. Expand into article/SKU setup + supplier onboarding on the same buyer → higher ACV; 80–115 retailers → £4–5M ARR.

07GTM / distribution

Channels

  • The paid audit as land — a "pay-if-it-finds-money" Exception-Loss Audit is the wedge that gets the first yes.
  • ERP app marketplaces (reuse the connector per platform).
  • Retail / supply-chain trade media, events, communities; chargeback/OTIF search intent.
  • Targeted outbound to mid-market retailers/distributors on a known ERP.

Motion & the GTM hire

  • Considered B2B sale; land via diagnostic, expand via suite (land-and-expand → high NRR).
  • One domain GTM hire with a retail supply-chain network shortens the second and third sales.
  • The reusable ERP connector compounds: cost-to-serve falls as you scale.

09Pilot / 2-week bake-off

  • Setup: read-only feed (PO/ASN/GRN/email) for the top-50 suppliers.
  • Run: agent detects exceptions, drafts/sends chases (or proposes them), tracks resolution.
  • Success metric: touchless-resolution rate ≥40–50% + a credible £ loss figure the buyer couldn't previously compute.
  • Green-light: the audit number + touchless rate justify the subscription.
