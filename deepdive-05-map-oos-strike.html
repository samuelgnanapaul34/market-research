MAP / Out-of-Stock Strike Agent

An agent that monitors a brand's products across retailers and marketplaces for minimum-advertised-price (MAP) violations and out-of-stocks, then acts — issuing violation notices, opening enforcement cases, building do-not-sell lists, and triggering restock workflows.

01The problem & the wedge

A brand selling through retailers and marketplaces bleeds margin two ways: MAP violations (resellers undercutting the advertised-price floor, eroding everyone's margin and triggering price wars) and out-of-stocks (lost sales + lost search rank). Today brands buy monitoring tools that surface the problem on a dashboard — and a human still does the enforcement and the restock chase.

  • MAP enforcement is a relentless, manual loop: spot the violation → match the reseller → send the notice → escalate → track.
  • Out-of-stocks bleed daily and compound (lost Buy Box, lost rank).
  • Monitoring is crowded; the "act" layer is thinner — most tools stop at the alert.
The wedge: "does the work, not dashboards." The agent doesn't just detect — it enforces (drafts + sends graduated MAP notices, opens cases, maintains do-not-sell lists) and triggers restock actions, closing the loop autonomously.

02ICP & buyer

Who buys

  • Champion + economic buyer: the brand's channel / eCommerce / marketplace manager (or brand-protection lead).
  • Firmographics: D2C / CPG / consumer-brand manufacturers with reseller networks selling on Amazon + marketplaces; revenue ~$10M–$1B.
  • Trigger: visible price erosion, channel conflict, rogue resellers, lost Buy Box.

Where they congregate (reachable)

  • D2C / Amazon-seller / brand-protection communities & media.
  • Strong search intent: "MAP enforcement", "MAP monitoring software", "stop resellers undercutting".
  • eCommerce agencies + brand-protection law firms as referral partners.

05Product & execution strategy

v1 — narrowest wedge

  • One marketplace (Amazon) + MAP enforcement only: detect violation → match reseller → draft + send graduated notice → track → escalate.
  • Add OOS detection + restock-trigger as the second loop.

Architecture & build

  • Marketplace data ingestion → violation/OOS detection → reseller identity matching → enforcement workflow (notice templates, case tracking, do-not-sell list) → restock action hooks.
  • Build difficulty: moderate; data acquisition + reseller matching are the hard parts.
  • Domain: channel/marketplace ops — codifiable.

06Roadmap & timeline

0–3 mo · Wedge. Amazon MAP detection + autonomous graduated enforcement; reseller matching; 3 design-partner brands; measure violations resolved.
3–6 mo · Close the loop. OOS detection + restock triggers; do-not-sell lists; more marketplaces; first paying brands.
6–12 mo · Scale. Multi-marketplace coverage; enforcement analytics; agency partnerships; 30–60 brands.
12–24 mo · Expand. Adjacent brand-protection (counterfeits, unauthorised sellers); 150–250 brands → £4–5M ARR.

07GTM / distribution

Channels (reachable)

  • Self-serve-friendly inbound — free "MAP violation scan" of a brand's ASINs as the lead magnet.
  • D2C / Amazon-seller communities & media; brand-protection law-firm + agency referrals.
  • Search-intent SEO around MAP enforcement.

Motion & the GTM hire

  • Lower-touch, faster sales cycle than the enterprise wedges (one stakeholder, shallow integration).
  • Land via free scan → show $ recovered → convert; expand to OOS + multi-marketplace.
  • The reachability is real but the field is crowded — differentiate on autonomy.

09Pilot / 2-week bake-off

  • Setup: a brand's ASIN/product list across its reseller network.
  • Run: agent detects MAP violations + OOS, matches resellers, drafts/sends graduated notices (or proposes), tracks responses.
  • Success metric: # violations detected & resolved + margin protected; OOS caught before lost-sales accrue.
  • Green-light: autonomous enforcement materially reduces violations vs the brand's status quo.
